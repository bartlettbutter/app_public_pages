We're here to help you get the most out of Barkpedia. Browse the answers below, or reach out directly if you can't find what you need.

## Getting Started

### How does breed identification work?

Barkpedia uses on-device machine learning to analyze your photo and identify the dog breed. All processing happens locally on your device, so your photos are never uploaded anywhere.

### Do I need an internet connection?

Breed identification and favorites work entirely offline, no internet required. However, breed details, the quiz, and dog trivia fetch content from the internet and do require a connection.

### Is Barkpedia free?

Yes, completely free. There are no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, and no ads.

### How many breeds can Barkpedia identify?

Barkpedia recognizes 147 breeds based on AKC standards.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why wasn't my dog's breed recognized correctly?

The model works best with clear, well-lit photos where the dog is the main subject. Try taking the photo from a different angle or in better lighting. Natural light is ideal, and front-facing or profile shots tend to work well. Mixed-breed dogs may show the closest matching purebred.

### Where is my data stored?

All scan history and favorites are stored locally on your device only. Nothing is sent to external servers.

### How do I delete saved favorites?

Swipe left on any item in the Favorites tab to delete it individually. To remove all app data at once, delete Barkpedia from your device.

### How do the Quiz and Trivia games work?

Both games require an internet connection. The Breed Quiz shows you a dog photo and asks you to pick the correct breed from four choices, with 10 questions per round. Dog Trivia presents true-or-false statements about dogs, also 10 questions per round. Both games award reward tiers based on your score.

### Does Barkpedia work on iPad?

Yes. Barkpedia is a universal app with an adaptive layout. It uses tab-based navigation on iPhone and sidebar navigation on iPad.

### Is my data private?

Yes. Your photos never leave your device and no personal data is collected. See our full [Privacy Policy](https://bartlettbutter.github.io/app_public_pages/privacy-policies/barkpedia/) for details.

## Permissions

### Why does Barkpedia need camera access?

Camera access lets you take photos of dogs directly in the app for breed identification. You can skip this permission and choose photos from your library instead.

### Why does Barkpedia need photo library access?

Photo library access lets you select existing photos from your library for breed identification. This is an alternative to using the camera.

### Does Barkpedia access my location or other data?

No. Barkpedia only requests camera and photo library access. It does not access your location, contacts, microphone, or any other sensitive information.

## Troubleshooting

### The camera isn't working

Open Settings, then navigate to Barkpedia and make sure Camera access is toggled on. If it's already enabled, try force-closing and reopening the app.

### Breed info, quiz, or trivia content isn't loading

These features require an active internet connection. Confirm you're connected to Wi-Fi or cellular data, then try again. If the issue persists, the service may be temporarily unavailable. Wait a few minutes and retry.

### The app says "Not a Dog"

Barkpedia first checks whether the photo contains a dog before attempting breed identification. If the image is too blurry, poorly lit, or the dog is too small in the frame, it may not detect a dog. Try a clearer photo with the dog as the main subject.

### The app is crashing or freezing

Make sure you're running the latest version from the App Store, then restart your device and try again. If the issue persists, please contact us below.

## Compatibility

Barkpedia requires iOS 17.0 or later and runs on both iPhone and iPad.
