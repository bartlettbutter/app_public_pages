# Nekopedia — Support

Welcome to the Nekopedia support page. Below you'll find answers to common questions and ways to get help.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How does breed identification work?

Nekopedia uses an on-device machine learning model (CoreML) to analyze photos and identify cat breeds. All processing happens locally on your device — your photos are never uploaded anywhere.

### Do I need an internet connection?

Breed identification works entirely offline using on-device machine learning. Saved favorites are also available offline. An internet connection is needed for breed details, the breed quiz, and cat trivia.

### Why wasn't my cat's breed recognized correctly?

The model works best with clear, well-lit photos where the cat is the main subject. Try taking the photo from a different angle or in better lighting. Mixed-breed cats may show the closest matching breed.

### Where is my data stored?

All scan history and favorites are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. Nothing is sent to external servers.

### How do I delete my saved favorites?

You can delete individual favorites by swiping left on them in the Favorites tab.

### How do the Quiz and Trivia games work?

Both games require an internet connection. The Breed Quiz shows you a cat photo and asks you to guess the breed from four choices. Cat Trivia presents true-or-false statements about cats. Each round is 10 questions, and you'll earn a reward title based on your score.

### Is Nekopedia free?

Yes, Nekopedia is completely free with no in-app purchases and no ads.

## Troubleshooting

### The camera isn't working

Make sure you've granted Nekopedia camera access in **Settings > Nekopedia > Camera**. If the issue persists, try restarting the app.

### The app is crashing

Please make sure you're running the latest version of Nekopedia from the App Store. If the problem continues, try restarting your device.

### Breed info, quiz, or trivia content isn't loading

These features require an internet connection. Check that your device is connected to Wi-Fi or cellular data.
